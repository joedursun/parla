# Duo — Your Personal Language Tutor

Duo is a private, conversational language learning app that mirrors the experience of having a dedicated personal tutor. Rather than gamified drills or multiple-choice quizzes, Duo teaches through natural conversation — the way people actually acquire languages.

Everything runs locally on your machine. Your conversations, progress, and data never leave your computer.

## Core Philosophy

**Conversation is the classroom.** The primary way you learn is by talking with your tutor. Lessons aren't worksheets — they're situated conversations (ordering food, asking for directions, negotiating a price) where vocabulary and grammar emerge naturally from context. The tutor adapts in real time: introducing new concepts when you're ready, circling back to weak spots, and gently correcting mistakes without breaking the flow.

**Corrections, not penalties.** When you make a mistake, the tutor handles it the way a good human teacher would — acknowledging what you got right, showing the correct form, and briefly explaining why. No red screens, no lost points. Just a nudge in the right direction.

**Review what you've learned.** Vocabulary and grammar from your conversations automatically feed into a spaced-repetition flashcard system. Cards are generated from things you've actually encountered — not a generic word list — so review always feels relevant to your experience.

**Know where you stand.** Progress tracking is ambient and honest. You can always see what you've mastered, what you're working on, and what's coming next. Weak areas are surfaced without judgment, along with suggestions for how to strengthen them.

## The Experience

### Getting Started

When you first open Duo, you choose your target language, self-assess your level, and tell the tutor what you're learning for (travel, work, conversation, culture, academics). This shapes the tutor's approach — a traveler preparing for a trip to Spain gets different lessons than someone studying for a certification exam.

### The Conversation

This is where you spend most of your time. You and the tutor have a back-and-forth conversation, either by typing or speaking. The tutor plays roles (a waiter, a shopkeeper, a new friend) to create realistic situations where the language comes alive.

What makes this different from just chatting with a chatbot:

- **New vocabulary is highlighted in context** as it appears, with meanings available on hover or in the side panel. You're never left guessing what a word means.
- **Grammar is taught in the moment.** When the tutor uses a new structure, a brief note appears in the context panel explaining the pattern. Grammar feels like a natural observation, not a lecture.
- **Corrections are inline and gentle.** If you write something that's understandable but not quite right, the tutor shows you the natural way to say it — with a short explanation of why.
- **Suggested responses** help when you're stuck. The tutor offers two or three things you could say next, with translations, so you're never frozen. As you progress, these scaffolds appear less often.
- **Voice is a first-class input.** You can hold the mic button to speak, and the tutor speaks back. Immersion means hearing and producing the language, not just reading it.
- **A context panel** beside the conversation shows the lesson focus, vocabulary introduced so far, grammar notes, and response suggestions — everything you need without leaving the conversation.

Each conversation has a topic and a loose structure, but the tutor follows your lead. If you ask an unexpected question or take the conversation in a new direction, the tutor goes with it and teaches from there.

### Flashcard Review

After each conversation, new vocabulary and grammar points are added to your review deck as flashcards. These aren't arbitrary — they're drawn directly from what you encountered.

The review system uses spaced repetition: cards you know well appear less often, while cards you struggle with come back sooner. When you review a card, you rate how easily you recalled it (Again / Hard / Good / Easy), and the system adjusts the schedule accordingly.

Cards show the word or phrase, an example sentence from the conversation where you first encountered it, pronunciation, and a translation. Audio playback lets you hear correct pronunciation.

You can also browse your full card collection, filter by topic or status (new, learning, mature), and manually add cards for anything you want to remember.

### Progress Tracking

The progress view gives you an honest picture of where you are:

- **Overall level** mapped to the CEFR framework (A1 through C2), with clear milestones showing what you've completed and what's ahead.
- **Skill breakdown** across reading, listening, writing, and speaking — because proficiency isn't one-dimensional.
- **Vocabulary by topic** showing mastery distribution. You can see at a glance which domains you're strong in and which need work.
- **Grammar concepts** tracked individually — which ones you've mastered, which you're practicing, and which are upcoming.
- **Weak areas** surfaced explicitly, with accuracy data, so you know exactly what to focus on.
- **Streak calendar** to keep you consistent. Not as a guilt mechanism — as a record of the work you're putting in.

### The Dashboard

The home screen brings everything together:

- A greeting in your target language
- One-tap access to continue your current lesson, review flashcards, start a free conversation, or do listening practice
- Stats at a glance (streak, words learned, practice time, accuracy)
- Your learning path showing lesson progression
- Recent vocabulary with strength indicators
- A reminder when flashcards are due

## How the Tutor Works

The tutor maintains a model of what you know. Every conversation updates this model:

- **What you've been taught** — vocabulary, grammar, and topics that have been introduced
- **What you've demonstrated** — things you've used correctly without prompting
- **What you struggle with** — errors, hesitations, and cards you frequently miss
- **What's next** — the tutor plans ahead, choosing topics and vocabulary that build logically on what you already know

This means two learners at the "same level" will have different experiences. The tutor adapts to you — your pace, your interests, your weak spots. It won't bore you by re-teaching what you've clearly mastered, and it won't rush past something you haven't absorbed yet.

The tutor also adjusts its language mix over time. Early on, explanations and interface text are mostly in your native language. As you advance, the tutor speaks more in the target language, progressively immersing you.

## Design Principles

1. **Conversation over curriculum.** Structure exists, but the conversation drives learning. The syllabus adapts to the learner, not the other way around.
2. **Gentle over punitive.** Mistakes are opportunities, not failures. The interface never makes you feel bad for not knowing something.
3. **Relevant over generic.** Everything you review comes from your own experience. Every example sentence is one you've actually seen.
4. **Ambient over intrusive.** Progress information is always available but never shoved in your face. You check it when you want to.
5. **Voice over text.** Language is spoken. Typing is a useful crutch, but the app always encourages you toward listening and speaking.
6. **Private by default.** Your data stays on your machine. No accounts, no cloud sync, no analytics. Just you and your tutor.
